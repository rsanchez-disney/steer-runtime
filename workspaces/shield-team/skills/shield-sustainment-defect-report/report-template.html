Executive Summary
📉 Trend Observed

The team peaked at 90 real defects in October 2025, driven by in-park testing that surfaced ~40 issues in a single week. Since then, a consistent downward trend has brought May 2026 to 46 real defects — a 49% reduction. The 3-month rolling average has stayed below the 12-month mean of 64 since January 2026, confirming structural improvement rather than seasonal variance. Notably, 17% of all tickets (161/933) were engineering work misclassified as bugs — stripping those out reveals an even cleaner decline.

🔍 Probable Root Causes
  • Recurring crashes filed per-release — CouchbaseLite expiry, TransactionTooLargeException, and Flutter lifecycle crashes generate new tickets each cycle instead of a single permanent fix. Accounts for ~14% of all real defects.
  • Finder / Map defects — Wayfinding and Map Finder surface ~95 defects over 12 months. A good improvement would be higher test coverage for map data and UI changes.
  • Misclassified engineering work — SonarQube compliance, CI/CD fixes, and Monorepo cleanups were routinely filed as Defects, inflating the apparent bug rate by 17%.
✅ Recommended Actions
  • Fix top-3 crash root causes permanently — CouchbaseLite migration, TransactionTooLargeException guard, and Flutter lifecycle audit. Estimated impact: −14% defect volume.
  • Automate tests for Finder/Map component — dedicated test regression suite per month to stop ~12% of monthly defects from recurring.
  • Enforce correct issue types — require Stories/Tasks for engineering work. Would immediately reduce visible defect count by ~17% and improve metric accuracy.
  • Monthly defect triage review — classify tickets at time-of-creation to avoid misclassification accumulating over quarters.

Key Insights
📉
Defects are trending down
Real defects peaked at 90 in Oct 2025 and have declined to 46 in May 2026 — a 49% reduction. The 3-month rolling average has been consistently below the annual average since Jan 2026.
🔬
SonarQube campaign inflated Feb–Mar
Feb 2026 (21 eng tasks) and Mar 2026 (35 eng tasks) saw a large number of SonarQube / Jacoco code-coverage tickets filed as "Defects". Stripping them reveals a steady decline in true bugs.
🔷
Sep–Oct 2025 spike from in-park testing
The September 2025 "in-park 2025" testing session flooded the backlog with ~40 issues in a single week. Many were duplicate observations across platform. Real unique issues were significantly fewer.
📊
17% of tickets should not be bugs
161 out of 933 tickets were engineering tasks (SonarQube, Grafana, Monorepo cleanup, CI/CD fixes, Vendomatic retirement, unit-test improvements) filed as Defect/Bug issue type.
💥
Crashes are a persistent category
NewRelic and Google Play Console crashes appear every month. Many are recurring root-cause patterns (Couchbase, Flutter engine lifecycle, TransactionTooLargeException) tracked as separate tickets each release.
🌍
Localization Phase 2 drove COREEXP in May
5 out of 55 May 2026 issues are Spanish translation gaps from the L10n Phase 2 rollout (COREEXP project). These are expected planned work, not regressions.
