## 1. Executive Summary

A deployment to Payment Service (BAPP0012692) on June 15, 2026 introduced a regression that caused payment gateway timeout failures across all WDW and DLR digital commerce platforms. The incident lasted approximately 24+ hours before a rollback was performed on June 16, 2026 at 20:53 EDT. The issue cascaded through multiple downstream services, resulting in widespread checkout failures and direct revenue impact.

## 3. Root Cause

**Primary Cause:** The June 15 deployment to Payment Service (BAPP0012692) introduced a regression that caused the service to timeout when communicating with the payment gateway.

**Most Probable Mechanism:** Connection pool exhaustion or TLS configuration change. The deployment likely altered either:
- Connection pool settings (max connections, idle timeout, or pool size), causing the service to exhaust available connections under normal load, OR
- TLS/SSL handshake configuration (cipher suite, certificate chain, or protocol version), causing handshake failures or excessive retry/timeout behavior against the payment gateway endpoint.

## 9. Lessons Learned

1. **Lack of payment-path synthetic monitoring** allowed a 24+ hour outage to persist without automated detection.
2. **No deployment validation gate** specific to payment gateway connectivity existed in the CI/CD pipeline.
3. **Cascade failure propagation** was not mitigated by circuit breakers or bulkheads between services.
4. **The blast radius of a single service deployment** affected the entire digital commerce platform across both WDW and DLR properties.
